Coyote's Mobile Apps

As Lead Product Designer for Coyote Logistics, I was in charge of designing an internal app used by employees to find information about shippers, carriers, and the freight they're trying to move as well as an external app used by shippers to find information about their freight and network. After numerous user interviews and researching similar apps in the marketplace, I designed the user interface and coded a front-end framework to implement the designs. This work was then built into PhoneGap to produce the final product.

Both apps have a similar look and feel and even share some features but the external app has a more streamlined user experience as it's trying to solve a smaller, more specific problem.

Client
Coyote Logistics

Mobile Apps

Designer, Front-End Developer

Grillo Group

Some clients know exactly what they want, and the Grillo Group absolutely had to have a horizontal scrolling website with an implemented content management system. In case you hadn't noticed, almost every website goes up and down for a reason—when the width of pages are unknown, it's difficult to manage the experience for users. Using JavaScript and HTML standards, I started with an already designed Photoshop file and coded the site to the exact look and feel the client was after. The site was built into a content management system which allows for easy updating by the client. Once I created a rough prototype I provided advice and insight on the site's user interface and animation to get it feeling and working just right.
